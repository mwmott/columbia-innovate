Columbia J-School Innovate

In this project, Columbia Journalism School Students are looking into how to encourage more student-driven innovation at the school. We hope to build an innovation lab to empower journalism students to learn new technologies, build start-ups and better prepare to enter the changing world of journalism.

Project Overview

Conversations about the future of journalism pop-up between classes and in the hallways of the journalism school, however there doesn't seem to be a space in the curriculem for students to work on projects toward this end. We hope to start an innovation lab at the school that is based in students developing projects to tell stories and help save and strengthen journalis online. We began with weekly conversations toward this goal, and have been speaking with the school's faculty to try and make this happen.

We'd like to end 2014 with a few long-term and short-term goals:

  • Short-term: We're working on two events in the Spring: One a several-day walkthrough of the basics of start-ups, and the other a team participating in an event by the Columbia Organization for Rising Entrepreneurs, a hackathon style competition, where several of our journalism students can partner with students from other schools to create a new news innovation or start-up.
  • Long-term: We hope to help develop infrastructure in the school that supports student innovation in spaces of journalism, technology and entrepreneurship. We're considering doing this through a program similar to the Stabile program at the school, where students work in the same classes together all year, and also the The Documentary Project, where students could spend a third year working on an innovation or start-up.
